# Citrus Fruits Overview

**Topic:** Fruit
**Sub-Topic:** Citrus

This document explores citrus fruits, a diverse group of fruit-bearing trees and shrubs known for their tangy flavor and high vitamin C content. Citrus fruits are among the most widely cultivated fruits globally, prized for both culinary and nutritional value.

### Common Citrus Varieties
- **Oranges:** Sweet and juicy, perfect for eating fresh or juicing.
- **Lemons:** Tart and acidic, essential for cooking and beverages.
- **Limes:** Similar to lemons but with distinctive flavor, used in cocktails and cuisine.
- **Grapefruits:** Large and slightly bitter, popular for breakfast.
- **Mandarins:** Small, easy to peel, including tangerines and clementines.

### Nutritional Benefits
Citrus fruits are excellent sources of vitamin C, supporting immune function and skin health. They provide dietary fiber, potassium, folate, and various antioxidants. Regular consumption may reduce risk of chronic diseases.

### Cultivation
Citrus trees thrive in subtropical and tropical climates with well-drained soil. They require adequate sunlight, water, and protection from frost. Major production regions include California, Florida, Brazil, and Mediterranean countries.

### Culinary Uses
Beyond fresh consumption, citrus adds flavor to desserts, marinades, dressings, and beverages. Zest provides concentrated citrus essence, while juice serves as both ingredient and drink.

### Conclusion
Citrus fruits combine delicious taste with impressive nutritional profiles, making them dietary staples worldwide and important agricultural commodities.
