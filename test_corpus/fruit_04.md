# Stone Fruits

**Topic:** Fruit
**Sub-Topic:** Stone Fruits

This document examines stone fruits, also called drupes, characterized by outer fleshy parts surrounding a pit or stone containing the seed. These fruits are beloved for their juiciness and sweet flavors.

### Common Stone Fruits
- **Peaches:** Fuzzy-skinned fruits with sweet, aromatic flesh in white or yellow varieties.
- **Plums:** Smooth-skinned fruits ranging from tart to sweet in various colors.
- **Cherries:** Small, round fruits growing in clusters, sweet or tart depending on variety.
- **Apricots:** Small orange fruits with velvety skin and mildly sweet flavor.
- **Nectarines:** Smooth-skinned peach relatives with similar taste and texture.

### Seasonality
Stone fruits thrive in temperate climates and are typically summer fruits. Fresh availability peaks from May through September in the Northern Hemisphere, though imports extend seasons.

### Nutritional Content
These fruits provide vitamins A and C, potassium, fiber, and antioxidants. Low in calories and fat, they support eye health, immunity, and digestive function.

### Preservation Methods
Beyond fresh consumption, stone fruits are canned, frozen, dried, or made into jams and preserves. Drying concentrates sugars, creating prunes from plums.

### Culinary Uses
Stone fruits excel in pies, tarts, cobblers, and compotes. They pair well with both sweet and savory dishes, especially grilled preparations.

### Conclusion
Stone fruits offer seasonal delights combining excellent nutrition with versatile culinary applications, making them summer favorites worldwide.
